Call For Papers

Coming Soon

IEEE URUCON 2026 Conference invites engineering professionals from industries, R&D organizations, academic institutions, government departments and research scholars, to submit original professional presentations and full length research contributions.

Submission

Submission Instructions

1
Prepare your manuscript
Use the double-column A4 IEEE Conference Template. Initial submissions must be up to 4 pages. Final accepted papers may include up to 5 pages. The official language is English, although papers in Spanish will also be accepted. Only final paper PDF manuscripts from registered authors (Authors Registration fee paid), English written, and accepted will be submitted to the IEEE Xplore Digital Library, as English is the world technical indexing lenguage.
2
Check formatting requirements
Verify margins are correctly set to prevent submission rejection. Ensure figures and graphics do not invade margins. Remove all embedded hyperlinks from the PDF before uploading. Include all co-authors in the same order as listed in the paper.
3
Create an EDAS account
All authors must create an EDAS account and be registered in the system before submission. This avoids issues during peer review and final paper upload.
4
Submit via EDAS
Upload your manuscript through the EDAS conference submission portal. Each author may upload up to two papers. One author registration covers the presentation of up to three papers at the conference.
5
Complete IEEE eCopyright (final submission)
Before uploading the final paper, the first author must complete the IEEE Electronic Copyright Form (ECF) through EDAS. Only papers from registered, fee-paid authors will be considered for IEEE Xplore indexing.
Evaluation

Review Process

Acceptance
Paper meets all requirements and is accepted for publication and presentation.
Minor Revision
Authors must address reviewers' suggestions in the final submission to be accepted.
Rejection
Paper does not meet the standards required for acceptance at this conference.
All submissions are subject to IEEE's plagiarism policy. Authors are encouraged to properly cite prior work to avoid high similarity rates. See the IEEE Plagiarism Policy for details.
